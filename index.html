Michaela (Mila) Hardt

I work at Medical Brain at Google. My goal is to use ML to help doctors deliver the right care to their patients at the right time. See our original paper, a blog post, our fhir github repository including a basic model that can be served on the Google Cloud Platform. I am specifically interested in aspects of fairness and interpretability and have written about the topic here. Before I worked at Twitter as a founding member of the timelines quality team, that introduced ML to the home timeline. I also worked on aspects of search quality including tracking and incorporating of realtime signals into the ranking function.

I graduated in 2012 with a PhD in Computer Science from Cornell University advised by Johannes Gehrke. Prior to this, I spent a semester at Carnegie Mellon University working with Christos Faloutsos on modeling blog dynamics. I obtained a B.S. in Computer Science at Saarland University in Saarbrücken, Germany.
